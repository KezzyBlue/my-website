# Competitive Programming

This is the first thing I learned when I first started learning programming and I very love it!!

🗺️ The beginning of an involuntary journey 🌱

Everything started when I was 12 years old, I was removed from the school's math team. But at that time I loved math very much, and being eliminated from the team made me very sad. Therefore, my teacher got me permission to join the school's computer science team, where there will be participation of mathematical thinking, perhaps she knew I had a passion for computers since I was little.

Here I learned about programming, and the first programming language I learned was Pascal. My first program was to calculate the sum of two numbers A and B that were entered. I felt like " It's really amazing and cool." I started to like it since that moment!

The first time I really competed using my knowledge was around April 2021 in the district-level competition, and of course, I didn't get any results at all. After that, I spent the entire summer vacation genuinely serious about this subject. At that time, there was no one to guide or instruct me, and I only self-study from internet. At the end of the summer vacation in 2021, I came back school with the new programming language, which is C++. And the knowledge I have only includes using basic functions, loops, and..... it's everything I have. And you know what? I actually survived in the exam room with nothing but those things and my own thinking, solving problems I had never seen before—like greatest common divisor, advanced array processing, and I even managed to come up with Bouble sort right there during the exam. As a result, I was selected for the district’s provincial gifted student team. This officially marked a new milestone in my journey.

📖 How did I self-study? 💪

After I was selected for the district’s provincial gifted student team, I studied at Lap Thach Secondary school for 3 months to prepare for the provincial-level gifted student competition.

Here, I had the opportunity to meet people who share the same passion and learnt about their learning and self-learning methods. After three months and the official competition, I was among the top performers on the team that year.

My self-learning methods:

  • Deep Thinking:. Instead of immediately looking for a solution by other when you see a difficult problem, why not try writing down your problem, breaking it down into its parts, and forcing your brain to work at full capacity using all the information within? I've realized that you only improve fastest when you push yourself to the limit.
  • Ask someone else:. Of course, when something is beyond your capabilities and you've tried your best, asking others for help is crucial. This not only helps us solve the problem but also allows us to learn how others think and handle, giving us a more nuanced perspective on an issue. In addition, participating in academic communities and forums is also very helpful.
  • Always be proactive:. Not everyone can answer your questions immediately, so you need to proactively learn from the internet, books, and reputable sources. (I think about it everywhere, even while eating, using the toilet, or taking a shower, just because of a problem I still can't solve. And ideas usually come at the most unexpected times 😅😅).
  • Don't rely too much on AI:. Luckily, during my self-study period, chatbots weren't as prevalent, and I was unaware of their existence. However, looking at the present, I see many young people abusing them, losing their ability to think deeply and critically.
